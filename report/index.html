  2. [2] J. Fuchs, F. Fischer, F. Mansmann, E. Bertini, and P. Isenberg, ‘Evaluation of alternative glyph designs for time series data in a small multiple setting’, in Proceedings of the SIGCHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems, New York, NY, USA, Apr. 2013, pp. 3237–3246. doi: 10.1145/2470654.2466443.
    Concept: This paper compared the performance of 4 glyph designs for time series data in a small multiple setting.
    Implementation:  They let participants conduct three tasks: (1) Peak Detection, (2) Temporal Location and (3) Trend Detection under different data density, on line, star, stripe and clock glyph design. They recorded the accuracy, efficiency and confidence of the tasks.
    Key Related Work:??
    Data Characteristics:  Temporal data. One dataset has 24 values, another set has 96 values. Analysis Technique: statistical (time, error and confidence)
    Evaluation Technique: Hypothesis test
    Results:For peak and trend detection, Line Glyph is better. For finding temporal location, Star Glyph and Clock Glyph are more effective.
    DOI: 10.1145/2470654.2466443
